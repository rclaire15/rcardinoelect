1.In your own words, what is the difference between SEO and SEM

SEO refers to the practice of refining your website to make it show up better in Google search results and provide a great experience to your visitors. SEM refers to the entire set of online advertising practices, which includes SEO, pay-per-click advertising (PPC), web design, and more.

2.Briefly discuss the following SEO Strategies listed:

*DOMAIN NAME STRATEGY: Domain names are the unique, human-readable Internet addresses of websites. They are made up of three parts: a top-level domain (sometimes called an extension or domain suffix), a domain name (or IP address), and an optional subdomain.Domain names are the second level of a domain's hierarchy (after the top-level domain). Domain names on a specific TLD (called a root domain, discussed below) are purchased from registrars and represent the specific, unique location of a website. In the following examples, the domain names are bolded: www.example.org https://moz.com www.blogspot.com

*LINKING STRATEGY: refers to the process of getting external pages to link to a page on your website. It is one of the many tactics used in search engine optimization (SEO).

*KEYWORD STRATEGY: are essential to developing winning search engine marketing campaigns. Your keyword strategy should involve selecting high-performing keywords that drive relevant traffic to your business. Choosing the right keywords for advertising can make all the difference in your campaigns, determining how well your advertisements rank on Google and other search engine platforms.

*TITLE TAGS STRATEGY: is an HTML element that specifies the title of a web page. Title tags are displayed on search engine results pages (SERPs) as the clickable headline for a given result, and are important for usability, SEO, and social sharing.

*META DESCRIPTION TAGS STRATEGY: in HTML is the 160 character snippet used to summarize a web page's content. Search engines sometimes use these snippets in search results to let visitors know what a page is about before they click on it.

*ALT TAGS STRATEGY: also known as "alt attribute" and "alt description," is an HTML attribute applied to image tags to provide a text alternative for search engines. Applying images to alt tags such as product photos can positively impact an ecommerce store's search engine rankings.

*SEARCH ENGINE INDEXING STRATEGY: A search engine can index documents against bi-words or n-words to get accurate results for phrase search. But indexing against n-words will increase the index size exponentially.
